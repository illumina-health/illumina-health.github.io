Research Themes

The lab's scientific agenda is organized around four interconnected pillars that reflect our core identity.

Medicine

Investigating translational pathways from molecular discovery to clinical practice, with emphasis on precision diagnostics, therapeutic innovation, and patient-centered outcomes research.

Translational Diagnostics Precision

Preventive Health

Developing data-driven strategies for disease prevention and population health promotion, integrating surveillance systems, behavioral science, and community-based intervention design.

Population Health Prevention Behavioral

Health Equity

Centering equity in every phase of the research process — from study design and data representation to algorithmic fairness and the distribution of health interventions across diverse communities.

Equity Fairness Social Determinants

Artificial Intelligence

Applying machine learning, deep learning, and computational modeling to accelerate discovery, optimize clinical workflows, and unlock insights from complex, multi-modal health data.

Machine Learning Deep Learning NLP

AI-Driven Health Equity Framework

A flagship initiative exploring how artificial intelligence can be designed, evaluated, and deployed in ways that actively reduce health disparities. The project examines algorithmic bias in clinical prediction models and proposes equity-aware evaluation protocols for health AI systems.

Preventive Health Intelligence Platform

Developing a computational framework for integrating real-time public health surveillance data with population-level risk models to support proactive, evidence-based disease prevention strategies at community and institutional scales.

Cross-Institutional Translational Research Network

Building a collaborative research infrastructure across Harvard Medical School, Rutgers Ernest Mario School of Pharmacy, and Duke University to enable seamless data sharing, joint analysis, and co-authored scholarship across institutional boundaries.

Ongoing Work

Active research streams and areas of investigation.

Algorithmic Fairness in Clinical Prediction

Examining how machine learning models used in clinical settings perform across different demographic groups, and developing corrective methods to improve equitable outcomes.

Preventive Health Data Integration

Building pipelines to combine electronic health records, public health surveillance data, and social determinants of health indicators into unified analytical frameworks.

AI for Drug Repurposing

Exploring computational approaches to identify existing therapeutics that may be effective for new indications, with emphasis on diseases disproportionately affecting underserved populations.
