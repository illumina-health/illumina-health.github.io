About

Our founding story, mission, and the principles that shape our research.

Who We Are

Illumina Health Lab is an interdisciplinary research initiative founded in 2026 through a collaboration between students and researchers affiliated with Harvard Medical School, Rutgers Ernest Mario School of Pharmacy, and Duke University. The lab was created with a singular ambition: to advance human health by fusing the rigor of academic medicine with the transformative potential of artificial intelligence, preventive health science, and equity-centered research design.

We are not a traditional single-department lab. Our structure is intentionally cross-institutional, reflecting our belief that the most impactful work happens when diverse perspectives, disciplines, and institutional strengths converge. Our founding members span medicine, pharmaceutical science, data science, and public health — forming a research collective uniquely positioned to tackle the interconnected challenges facing modern healthcare systems.

From the outset, the lab has been guided by a commitment to producing research that is both scientifically rigorous and socially accountable — work that does not merely describe health challenges, but actively contributes to solving them.

Our Mission

To transform the landscape of health research by building an interdisciplinary platform where translational medicine, preventive and population health, equitable healthcare systems, and AI-enabled discovery operate as integrated pillars of a single scientific vision.

We pursue research questions that sit at the boundaries between disciplines — where clinical insight meets computational power, where equity principles inform algorithmic design, and where preventive strategies are grounded in molecular and population-level evidence. Our goal is to develop methods, tools, and frameworks that can be adopted by healthcare institutions, public health agencies, and research communities worldwide.

At the Intersection

Our work is defined by the convergence of four critical domains.

Translational Medicine

Bridging laboratory discovery and clinical application to improve diagnostics, therapeutics, and patient outcomes at every stage of the research-to-care pipeline.

Preventive & Population Health

Developing proactive, data-informed strategies for disease prevention and health promotion that operate at individual, community, and systemic levels.

Equitable Healthcare Systems

Ensuring that research design, data collection, algorithmic tools, and clinical applications are built to serve all populations — especially those historically underserved.

AI-Enabled Research

Leveraging machine learning, deep learning, and computational modeling to extract meaningful insights from complex biomedical and health systems data at scale.

Founding Vision

Illumina Health Lab was conceived as more than a research group — it is an institutional experiment in what becomes possible when talented, mission-driven individuals from different academic traditions choose to work together on shared problems. Founded by students and researchers across three of the world's leading academic institutions, the lab represents a new model of collaborative scholarship: one built on mutual respect, intellectual ambition, and a deep sense of responsibility toward the communities our work is meant to serve.

As we grow, we remain anchored in the principles that brought us together — a belief that health research should be interdisciplinary by design, equitable in its impact, and bold in its vision for what science can achieve.
